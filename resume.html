Craft a Resume that Tells Your Story

Your resume is often your first impression—an opportunity to highlight your unique strengths, skills, and experiences to potential employers.
Whether you’re starting from scratch or looking to polish an existing resume, these resources are here to help you every step of the way.

UMSI students are in high demand, and a clear, well-organized resume will help you stand out among applicants. On this page, you'll find
practical tips, templates, and guides—all tailored to the needs of School of Information students and aligned with employer expectations.

Resume Writing Made Easy: Steps to Success

  1. Download a UMSI-Approved Template:

    2. Start with a format that works—no need to design from scratch.

    Find Templates here

  2. Gather Your Experiences:

    3. List jobs, internships, volunteer roles, coursework, and projects most relevant to your goals:

    1. Jobs:
      2. Include part-time or full-time positions where you gained relevant skills or industry exposure.

      Example: Marketing Assistant at XYZ Corp – Managed social media campaigns and analyzed customer engagement data.

    2. Internships:
      3. List internships (paid or unpaid) that offered hands-on experience in your desired field.

      Example: Software Engineering Intern at ABC Tech – Contributed to mobile app development using React Native.

    3. Volunteer Roles:
      4. Highlight volunteer experiences that helped build transferable skills or align with your mission.

      Example: Volunteer ESL Tutor – Provided weekly English lessons to non-native speakers, improving communication skills.

    4. Coursework:
      5. Mention specific classes (especially upper-level or specialized) that gave you relevant theoretical or technical knowledge.

      Example: "Data Structures & Algorithms" – Built a foundation in efficient coding and problem-solving.

    5. Projects:
      6. Describe personal, academic, or collaborative projects that demonstrate your skills or initiative.

      Example: Capstone Project – Developed a predictive analytics tool using Python and machine learning.

  3. Write Impactful Bullets:

    4. Use action verbs and highlight your skills, results, and unique contributions:

    For Example:
    • Leadership: Led, Coordinated, Directed, Oversaw, Managed
    • Communication: Presented, Wrote, Facilitated, Advised, Collaborated
    • Technical Skills: Developed, Coded, Engineered, Designed, Analyzed
    • Problem Solving: Resolved, Improved, Streamlined, Optimized, Diagnosed
    • Research/Analysis: Investigated, Evaluated, Assessed, Compiled, Modeled

    Also, employers and admissions committees love to see measurable outcomes and the impact of your work.

    Ask yourself: What changed because of what I did?

    Use numbers, percentages, or tangible results when possible.

    Example:

    Led a team of 4 to develop a social media strategy that increased follower growth by 30% in 3 months.

  4. Tailor for Each Opportunity:

    5. Customize your resume for every job or internship you pursue.

    Applying to more than one job?

    Try this resource that utilizies AI to tailor your resume to job discriptions

  5. Review and Edit:

    6. Proofread for errors, check formatting, and ask for feedback from peers or a career coach.

Resume FAQ

  • How long should my resume be?
    For most UMSI students and new grads, one page is best. More experienced professionals may require two pages.
  • Do I need a different resume for each application?
    It’s wise to tailor your resume to the requirements and keywords of each opportunity.
  • Is it okay to include coursework or student projects?
    Absolutely! UMSI coursework and projects are valued by employers, especially if they show relevant skills and impact.
  • How often should I update my resume?
    Update your resume every semester, after major projects, or whenever you gain a new role or responsibility.
